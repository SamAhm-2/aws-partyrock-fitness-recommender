## 🧠 What is this app?

This is an AI-powered fitness recommender built on **Amazon PartyRock** that suggests personalized workout routines based on your current energy level. Instead of following a rigid schedule, it meets you where you are — whether you're feeling pumped and ready to push hard, or low-energy and just want to move gently.

You describe how you feel. The AI figures out what workout fits.

## ✨ Features

- 🔋 **Energy-based recommendations** — input your energy level and get a matching workout
- 🏋️ **Personalized suggestions** — tailored to your mood, time available, and fitness goals
- 💬 **Conversational AI interface** — just describe how you feel in plain language
- ⚡ **Instant results** — no sign-up, no complicated input forms

## 🛠️ How I Built This (No Code!)

This app was built entirely using **[Amazon PartyRock](https://partyrock.aws)** — AWS's no-code generative AI playground powered by Amazon Bedrock.

### What is PartyRock?

PartyRock lets anyone build AI-powered apps by:
- Connecting **widgets** (text inputs, AI outputs, images, etc.)
- Writing **natural language prompts** to instruct the AI
- Zero coding or cloud setup required

### My Building Process

**Step 1 — Define the idea**  
I started with a simple problem: *fitness apps don't adapt to how you actually feel day-to-day.* I wanted an app that takes your energy as the starting point.

**Step 2 — Design the widgets**  
In PartyRock, I set up:
- A **text input widget** where users describe their energy level
- An **AI text output widget** that generates a workout recommendation
- Prompt logic connecting the two

**Step 3 — Write the prompt**  
The core of the app is the AI prompt I wrote. Prompt engineering (crafting instructions for the AI) is the main "skill" involved. My prompt tells the AI to:
- Understand the user's energy level from their description
- Recommend an appropriate workout type and duration
- Keep the tone encouraging and realistic

**Step 4 — Test and iterate**  
I tested with different energy descriptions ("exhausted," "moderate," "high energy") and refined the prompt until the recommendations felt genuinely useful and varied.
